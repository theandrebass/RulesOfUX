01

Users often perceive aesthetically pleasing design as design that’s more usable. Aesthetically pleasing design can make users more tolerant of minor usability issues.

The Aesthetic-Usability Effect

Kate Moran | Nielsen Norman Group

The Aesthetic-Usability Effect

Abhishek Chakraborty | Medium

02

Productivity soars when a computer and its users interact at a pace (<400ms) that ensures that neither has to wait on the other.

Provide system feedback within 400ms in order to keep users’ attention and increase productivity.

This 70s UX gem still applies

Michael Gugel | Medium

Economic Value Of Rapid Response

Jim Elliott

03

The time to acquire a target is a function of the distance to and size of the target.

Make elements you wish to be easily selectable large and position them close to users.

Fitts’s Law

Interaction Design Foundation

04

The time it takes to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices.

UI/UX Psychology Principles

Thanasis Rigopoulos | Marvel

Hick’s Law

Interaction Design Foundation

05

Users spend most of their time on other sites. This means that users prefer your site to work the same way as all the other sites they already know.

You can simplify the learning process for users by providing familiar design patterns.

Jakob’s Law of Internet UX

06

Elements tend to be perceived into groups if they are sharing an area with a clearly defined boundary.

Adding borders (creating common regions) around an element or group of elements is an easy way to create separation from surrounding elements.

07

People will perceive and interpret ambiguous or complex images as the simplest form possible, because it is the interpretation that requires the least cognitive effort of us.

The Principles of Gestalt

Interation Design Foundation

08

Objects that are near, or proximate to each other, tend to be grouped together.

UI/UX Psychology Principles

Thanasis Rigopoulos | Marvel

The Principles of Gestalt

Interation Design Foundation

09

The human eye tends to perceive similar elements in a design as a complete picture, shape, or group, even if those elements are separated.

The Principles of Gestalt

Interation Design Foundation

Design Principles

Steven Bradley | Smashing Magazine

10

Elements that are visually connected are perceived as more related than elements with no connection.

Principles of Perception

Andy Rutledge

The Principles of Gestalt

Interation Design Foundation

11

The average person can only keep 7 (plus or minus 2) items in their working memory.

Chunking is an effective method of presenting groups of content in a manageable way.

Miller’s Law

Khan Academy

12

Among competing hypotheses that predict equally well, the one with the fewest assumptions should be selected.

Analyze each element and remove as many as possible, without compromising the overall function.

Designing with Occam’s Razor

Jon Yablonski | Medium

Occam’s Razor

Interaction Design Foundation

13

The Pareto principle states that, for many events, roughly 80% of the effects come from 20% of the causes.

Focus the majority of effort on the areas that will bring the largest benefits to the most users.

The 80/20 Rule

Arin Bhowmick | Medium

The Pareto Principle

Interaction Design Foundation

14

Be liberal in what you accept, and conservative in what you send.

Be flexible and tolerant to any actions the user could possibly take. This means accepting variable input from users, translating input to meet the requirements, defining boundaries for input, and providing clear feedback to the user.

Postel’s Law

Mark Boulton

15

The Von Restorff effect, also known as The Isolation Effect, predicts that when multiple similar objects are present, the one that differs from the rest is most likely to be remembered.

UI/UX Psychology Principles

Thanasis Rigopoulos | Marvel

16

People remember uncompleted or interrupted tasks better than completed tasks.

Use progress bars for complex tasks to visually indicate when a task is incomplete, and thus increase the likelyhood it will be completed.

The Zeigarnik Effect

Abhishek Chakraborty | Medium
